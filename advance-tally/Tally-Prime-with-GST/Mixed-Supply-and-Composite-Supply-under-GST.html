Detailed Concept & Legal Logic under GST

Under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) framework, transactions may involve multiple goods, services, or a combination of both. GST classifies such transactions into two categories: Composite Supply and Mixed Supply.

Correct identification of these supplies determines the applicable GST rate, reporting method, and tax calculation.

Composite Supply is taxed according to the Principal Supply rate, while Mixed Supply is taxed at the highest GST rate applicable among all items in the bundle.

Difference Between Composite Supply and Mixed Supply

Attribute Composite Supply Mixed Supply
Legal Definition Two or more naturally bundled supplies supplied together in ordinary business practice. Two or more independent supplies combined together for a single price without being a composite supply.
Nature of Bundle Naturally bundled with one Principal Supply and supporting supplies. Artificially bundled items that can be sold separately.
Pricing May have individual prices or a combined price. Always supplied for one consolidated price.
Tax Rate Principal Supply GST rate applies to the entire value. Highest GST rate among all items applies to the entire value.

Composite Supply Examples

1. Machinery Sale with Freight and Transit Insurance

A manufacturer sells machinery worth ₹1,00,000 and charges mandatory freight and insurance of ₹5,000.

Freight and insurance are dependent on the machinery supply and cannot exist independently. Therefore, machinery is the Principal Supply.

Tax Treatment

Entire value of ₹1,05,000 is taxed at the machinery GST rate of 18%.

2. Hotel Stay with Complimentary Breakfast

A hotel provides accommodation with free breakfast as part of the room package. The accommodation service is the Principal Supply and breakfast is ancillary.

3. Printing Services

When a publisher provides content and a printer provides paper, ink, and printing services, the main supply is printing service. The transaction is treated as a service supply.

Mixed Supply Examples

1. Festive Gift Hamper

A Diwali gift hamper contains chocolates, dry fruits, aerated drinks, and diyas sold together for one price.

Each product can be purchased independently, making the bundle an artificial combination.

Tax Treatment

The highest GST rate in the bundle applies. Example: Aerated drinks at 28% determine the tax rate for the complete hamper.

2. Detergent Powder with Free Bucket

Detergent powder and a plastic bucket are separate products. Since they can be sold independently, the package is treated as a Mixed Supply.

3. Corporate Executive Gift Set

A combination of a watch, wallet, tie, and pen sold together is a Mixed Supply because these items do not naturally depend on each other.

Practical Recording in TallyPrime

Part A: Composite Supply Procedure

In Composite Supply, additional charges such as freight are added to the taxable value of the Principal Supply, and the Principal Supply GST rate applies to the complete value.

Step 1: Configure Service Ledger

  • Go to Gateway of Tally > Create/Alter > Ledger.
  • Create ledger: Freight & Forwarding Charges.
  • Set GST applicability as Not Applicable.
  • Enable inclusion in assessable value calculation for GST.
  • Select Appropriate to: Goods.
  • Select calculation method: Based on Value.

Step 2: Record Composite Sales Voucher

  • Open Sales Voucher using F8.
  • Select customer details.
  • Enter Principal Stock Item details.
  • Add Freight & Forwarding Charges ledger.
  • Select CGST and SGST for intra-state or IGST for inter-state supply.

Step 3: Verify Tax Calculation

Use Tax Analysis in TallyPrime to confirm that GST is calculated on the combined assessable value.

Example Calculation

Machinery Value: ₹1,00,000
Freight: ₹5,000
Total Taxable Value: ₹1,05,000
GST Rate: 18%
GST Amount: ₹18,900

Part B: Mixed Supply Procedure

For Mixed Supply, create a combined kit item using Bill of Materials (BOM) and apply the highest GST rate among the components.

Step 1: Enable BOM

  • Open Stock Item configuration.
  • Enable Set Components List (Bill of Materials).
  • Enable additional descriptions if required.

Step 2: Create Mixed Supply Kit

  • Create individual stock items with GST details.
  • Create combo item such as Festive Gift Kit.
  • Create BOM named Diwali Combo.
  • Add component items like Watch, Wallet, and Drink.
  • Assign the highest GST rate to the kit.

Step 3: Stock Journal Assembly

  • Open Stock Journal using Alt+F7.
  • Select Manufacturing Journal mode.
  • Select finished product and BOM.
  • Enter quantity to assemble.
  • Save the transaction.

Step 4: Record Mixed Supply Sale

  • Create Sales Voucher.
  • Select the Mixed Supply Kit.
  • Enter quantity and rate.
  • Apply CGST/SGST or IGST.
  • GST is calculated at the highest applicable rate.

Statutory Reporting and Return Impact

GSTR-1 Impact

  • Reported under outward supplies.
  • Composite Supply is reported under the Principal Supply HSN/SAC code.
  • Freight becomes part of the principal item's taxable value.
  • Mixed Supply is reported under the kit HSN/SAC code.
  • The highest tax rate is applied to the complete package value.

GSTR-3B Impact

  • Total taxable value includes the complete invoice amount.
  • GST amount is calculated according to Composite or Mixed Supply rules.

Troubleshooting and Configuration Fixes

Freight Ledger Applying Wrong GST Rate

Ensure Freight Ledger GST applicability is set to Not Applicable and included in assessable value calculation for GST.

Incorrect Place of Supply

Verify party state details and select CGST/SGST for intra-state transactions and IGST for inter-state transactions.

Incorrect Mixed Supply Stock Consumption

Check BOM configuration, component quantities, and Manufacturing Journal settings to ensure correct stock deduction.
